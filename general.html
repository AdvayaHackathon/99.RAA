General Medicine

What is General Medicine?

General Medicine, also known as Internal Medicine, is the medical specialty dealing with the prevention, diagnosis, and non-surgical treatment of diseases affecting adults. Practitioners are skilled in managing patients who have undifferentiated or multi-system disease processes.

The Role of a General Practitioner (GP)

General Practitioners are often the first point of contact for patients seeking medical attention. They provide comprehensive care, manage chronic illnesses, promote health and well-being, and coordinate care with specialists when necessary. They build long-term relationships with patients, understanding their health history and needs.

  • Diagnosis and treatment of acute illnesses
  • Management of chronic conditions
  • Preventive care and health screenings
  • Patient education and counseling
  • Referrals to specialists
